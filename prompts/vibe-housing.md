Build a full single-page landing page for a residential real estate agency called **Vibe Housing**.
Use semantic HTML and CSS in a single file.

## Visual Identity
Fresh, approachable, and modern — built for buyers and sellers across all price ranges. Use a clean
sans-serif typeface (e.g. Inter or DM Sans) for body and a slightly more characterful display face
(e.g. Cabinet Grotesk or Syne) for headlines. Palette should feel bright and trustworthy without
being corporate. Think Airbnb meets a boutique local agency.

## Sections (in order)

1. **Hero** — Full-viewport with a bold headline, short subheading, and a property search bar
   (fields: location, property type, min–max price) with a "Browse listings" CTA button.

2. **Featured listings** — A 3-column card grid showcasing 6 fictional properties. Each card
   includes a placeholder image (CSS-styled), property address, price, and key stats (beds, baths,
   sqm). Cards should have a subtle hover lift effect.

3. **Services** — Three service tiles: Buying, Selling, and Renting. Each has an icon, a short
   title, and a 2-sentence description of what Vibe Housing offers for that journey.

4. **Market stats** — A clean data strip with 4 headline numbers relevant to the local market:
   e.g. average sale price, days on market, properties sold this month, client satisfaction rate.
   Large typographic numbers with small labels beneath.

## Design Details
- **Palette:** fresh white `#FFFFFF`, soft sky `#EEF4FF`, vibrant cobalt `#2D5BE3`,
  warm charcoal `#1C1C1E`, mid grey `#6B7280`
- Sticky nav with logo left, "Browse listings" CTA button right
- Property cards use `border-radius: 12px` with a light border and hover shadow
- Market stats section uses cobalt as a background accent
- Smooth scroll and subtle fade-in on scroll for each section
- Fully responsive down to mobile — search bar stacks vertically on small screens
